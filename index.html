My Commitment & Action

As a student, I have directly experienced the pervasive fear and apprehension caused by ragging. This personal encounter ignited a deep passion to dismantle this harmful culture within educational institutions, ensuring new students can thrive in a safe and nurturing environment.

The scale of this issue is staggering and demands urgent attention:

  • Over the past 14 years, nearly 10,000 cases of ragging have been reported on India's centralized anti-ragging portal.
  • More alarmingly, between April 2022 and 2025 alone, 51 ragging-related deaths have occurred, a figure that tragically parallels student suicides in major coaching hubs.
  • Medical colleges, despite their smaller student populations, disproportionately account for a high percentage of these incidents and fatalities, reporting up to 30 times more ragging cases relative to student numbers compared to other institutes.

My practical approach to this issue was exemplified during our college's Tech Fest, where I led a team of over 50 student volunteers. I consciously adopted a guidance-based, non-dictatorial leadership style, demonstrating that even under intense pressure, significant achievements are possible through empathy and collective effort. This experience served as a tangible precedent, proving that a culture of mutual respect and compassion can effectively counter traditional power dynamics.

My Vision

My vision extends beyond simply preventing incidents; it's about fostering a fundamental cultural shift where empathy and dignity are paramount. The persistence of ragging is a symptom of outdated power structures and a lack of understanding regarding its profound psychological and physical impact.

I am dedicated to championing environments where students feel safe to express themselves, innovate, and contribute without fear of harassment. This involves continuous advocacy, leading by example, and inspiring others to embrace a more compassionate and collaborative approach to community building within educational settings.

The Movement Against Ragging

2001: Supreme Court Intervention

The Supreme Court of India takes suo motu cognizance of ragging incidents, leading to the first major judicial intervention against the practice in educational institutions.

2009: UGC Regulations

University Grants Commission (UGC) implements comprehensive regulations on curbing ragging in higher educational institutions, making it mandatory for colleges to prevent and address ragging.

2012: National Anti-Ragging Helpline

Launch of the 24x7 National Anti-Ragging Helpline (1800-180-5522) and online portal for students to report incidents anonymously.

2025: Current Advocacy

Student-led initiatives like Satyajit's demonstrate that cultural change through positive peer leadership can be more effective than punitive measures alone.
